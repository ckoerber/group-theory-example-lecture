IV The highest-weight construction scheme

IV.2 Roots and weights for $su(3)$

Gell-Mann matices

  • The Gell-Mann matrices are defined by

    $$\scriptsize \begin{aligned} \lambda_{1}&=\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} & \lambda_{2}&=\begin{pmatrix} 0 & -i & 0 \\ i & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} & \lambda_{3}&=\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} & \lambda_{4}&=\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \\ \lambda_{5}&=\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 & -i \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \\ i & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} & \lambda_{6}&=\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} & \lambda_{7}&=\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -i \\ 0 & i & 0 \end{pmatrix} & \lambda_{8}&=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -2 \end{pmatrix} \end{aligned} $$

  • They can be viewed as a generalization of the Pauli matrices to three dimensions width

    $\lambda_{a}=\begin{pmatrix} \sigma_{a} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$ for $a =1,2,3$

  • The $su(3)$ algebra can be defined by identifying a 3-dimensional representation with these matrices

    $D^{(3d)}(\hat \Lambda_a) = \lambda_a / 2$ for $\hat \Lambda_a \in su(3)$

    The structure constants are defined by the matrix commutator of these matrices and the Killing form simplifies to $\small \mathrm{tr}\left[D^{(3d)}(\hat \Lambda_a)D^{(3d)}(\hat \Lambda_b)\right] = \delta_{ab}/2$

  • The rank of $su(3)$ is two and we can thus diagonalize two generators at once.
    It is convenient to choose the general $su(3)$ Cartan subalgebra

    $C = \left\{ \hat H_1 \equiv \hat T_3 , \hat H_2 \equiv \hat T_8\right\}$

    In the above representation, these matrices are already diagonal.

$SU(3)$ Roots & weights

$$\footnotesize C = \left\{ \hat H_1 \rightarrow \frac{1}{2} \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} , \hat H_2 \rightarrow \frac{1}{2\sqrt{3}}\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -2 \end{pmatrix}\right\}$$

  • We can compute the eigenvalues of matrices in the Cartan subalgebra to obtain the weights of this representation

    $\small \hat H_i |\mathrm{3d}; \vec \mu^{[j]} \rangle = \mu^{[j]}_i |\mathrm{3d}; \vec \mu^{[j]} \rangle \Leftrightarrow D^{(3d)}(\hat H_i) \bold{v}^{[j]} = \mu^{[j]}_i \bold{v}^{[j]} $

    1. $\small \bold{v}^{[1]}=\begin{pmatrix} 1, & 0, & 0 \end{pmatrix}^T \Rightarrow \vec{\mu}^{[1]}=\frac{1}{2}\begin{pmatrix} +1, & \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \end{pmatrix}^T $

    2. $\small \bold{v}^{[2]}=\begin{pmatrix} 0, & 1, & 0 \end{pmatrix}^T \Rightarrow \vec{\mu}^{[2]}=\frac{1}{2}\begin{pmatrix} -1, & \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \end{pmatrix}^T $

    3. $\small \bold{v}^{[3]}=\begin{pmatrix} 0, & 0, & 1 \end{pmatrix}^T \Rightarrow \vec{\mu}^{[3]}=-\begin{pmatrix} +0, & \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \end{pmatrix}^T $

  • Roots must take one weight vector to another weight vector.
    We can take differences of weight vectors to find roots.

    1. $\vec{\alpha}^{[1]}=\vec{\mu}^{[1]}-\vec{\mu}^{[3]}=\frac{1}{2}\begin{pmatrix} 1, & +\sqrt{3}\end{pmatrix}^T$
    2. $\vec{\alpha}^{[2]}=\vec{\mu}^{[3]}-\vec{\mu}^{[2]}=\frac{1}{2}\begin{pmatrix} 1, & -\sqrt{3} \end{pmatrix}^T$
    3. $\vec{\alpha}^{[3]}=\vec{\mu}^{[1]}-\vec{\mu}^{[2]}$
    • Some roots are linearly dependent $\vec{\alpha}^{[3]}=\vec{\alpha}^{[1]}+\vec{\alpha}^{[2]}$ or $\vec{\alpha}^{[1]}=-\vec{\alpha}^{[7]}$
    • For some of the root vectors, raising $\hat H_1$ components lowers $\hat H_2$ components and vice versa
