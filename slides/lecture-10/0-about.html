Group Theory in Nuclear and Particle Physics Example Presentation

These slides were presented in the weekly "Group Theory in Nuclear and Particle Physics" lecture (this was the 10th lecture) at Ruhr-University Bochum during the winter semster 21/22. The lecture is a masters level course for physicists and mathematicians. This page demonstrates how this framework was used.

  • These slides contain dynamic elements such as
    • a voice over (recorded during the digital "live" lecture)
    • animated figures
    • white-board like derivations in form of videos
  • In particular, you can navigate using the arrow keys (←, ↑, →, and ↓) and start/stop the voice over by pressing "a"
    • This presentation is two-dimensional, going right changes sections, going down brings more details
    • Pressing "?" presents more controls over the slides
  • They are based on the reveal.js framework, were created using open-source tools, only, and are under version control. See also
