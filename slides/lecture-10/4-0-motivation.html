IV The highest-weight construction scheme

IV.0 Motiation

Motiation

How can we construct irreducible representations for general simple Lie algebras?

(From now on, I will refer to "simple Lie algebras" when saying "algebra")

  • For $su(2)$, we have used the highest-weight construction scheme:
    1. Find the state with largest $\hat J_3$ eigenvalue $|j, m\rangle$ with $m = j$
    2. Succesively apply the lowering operator $\hat J^-$ to find other states in this representation
    3. Repeat for orthogonal states to obtain the entire basis (other irreps.)

  • If a Lie algebra has higher rank, we can diagonalize two or more generators $\hat H_i$ and thus have more eigenvalues which define a basis

    $\hat{H}_{i}|D; \vec \mu \rangle = \mu_i |D; \vec \mu \rangle $ for $i=1, \cdots , N$

    (From now on $\mu \equiv \vec \mu$)

  • In this case, the action of lowering operators $\hat X^{-}_a$ may affect several eigenvalues at ones

    $$ \hat X^{-}_a |D; \vec \mu \rangle = |D; \vec \mu - \vec x_a \rangle$$

    This action could potentially lower one but raise another eigenvalue

    • What is a "highest eigenvalue"?
    • How can we ensure to find "all" states?

The highest-weight construction scheme

The generalized scheme for finding all irreps of a given representation of the algebra $A$ will follow the notion we applied to representations of $su(2)$

(As a matter of fact, we will find many sub-$su(2)$s to help us; more on this later)

  1. Find the maximal set of linearly* independent generators which commute (simultaneously diagonalizable)

    $$\left[\hat{H}_{i}, \hat{H}_{j}\right]=0, \quad \forall \hat{H}_{i}, \hat{H}_{j} \in C \subset A$$

  2. Find the highest-weight state for a given representation (largest eigenvalue for all generators in $C$)

    $$ |D; \mu_{\max{}}\rangle \equiv \left|D; \max \left(\mu_{1}\right), \max \left(\mu_{2}\right), \cdots, \max \left(\mu_{m}\right) \right\rangle $$

  3. Succesively apply all combinations of existing lowering and raising operators to this state to find the irrep

    $$ |D; \vec \mu_{\max{}} - \vec x_a + \vec x_b + \cdots \rangle = \cdots \hat X_b^+ \hat X_a^-|D; \vec \mu_{\max{}}\rangle $$

    The eventual scheme will tell us how to systematically find all states; this is not entirely "random".
